The Departure

They’d had an argument the day before and now he couldn’t stop thinking about it. No matter how many times James Jackson tried to think about other women, and there were thousands of former sexual partners to choose from, he could not her get out of his mind. Even singing along to the radio didn’t help.

Another hour’s drive down the dirt road brought him to a stopping point from which he still had a two-mile hike through the forest. With an overloaded backpack and a fiberglass kayak lifted onto his shoulders, J.J. began his trek to the lake. Up and down mountainsides and through thick forests, he navigated to his destination.

During the hike, his last conversation with Carrina Hewitt, his best friend, continued to replay in his mind. He had asked her out. Yet again. And she had refused him. Yet again. What had really thrown him for a loop was that she had told him how he could remedy the problem.

Three hours later he summited a peak and there it was. The lake. He was now only another six hundred feet away from where he would spend the next three weeks in peaceful solitude.

After setting up his campsite, he grabbed his fishing gear and paddled out to the center of the lake to do some fishing. With his solar powered radio tuned to a local pop music station, he baited his hook and cast his line into the water. For two hours he sat there catching fish and listening to music, but something had caught him as well. On the radio came the silky voice of Kylie Minogue singing Aphrodite. This wasn’t the first time he had heard the song, but one specific part hooked his attention. Kylie sang “You know that I'm magical. I am the original. I am the only one to make you feel this way. The moment that you kiss me you know that you'll miss me I am the only one.” His thoughts once again returned to Carrina. She was the only one he had ever truly desired and he longed for the day when he would be allowed to kiss her. But, she was the only one he couldn’t have.

As he paddled back to shore, he noticed some very unpleasant looking clouds starting to form over the lake. Right where his campsite was. The weather report he had gotten before he left home that morning had said there was a zero percent chance of rain for at least the next ten days. He generally trusted the weather forecast while he was on his trips, but these clouds looked like they had plans of their own.

After dinner, as he sat there listening to the music of the forest, a very disturbing occurrence dripped onto his cheek. It looked like the clouds had decided to follow through on their threat after all. Dowsing the campfire, he grabbed what was left of his cocoa and went into his tent. As soon as he had zipped it closed, the wind started to howl and the sky let loose with a spectacular fireworks display of thunder and lightning.

As the wind blew harder and the rain fell sideways, he realized that sleeping in the tent would not be an option at all that night. Rolling up his sleeping bag and stuffing it into his backpack he climbed out of his tent and made his way to a cave he had discovered during his hike in.

His face stung as the rain pelted him from every direction. The storm must have been directly overhead because there was no time delay between the lightning and the thunder. Lightning was striking the ground in rapid fire patterns all around him. How could the weatherman have missed this storm, he thought?

When he reached the cave, he took off his pack and set it on the ground several yards inside to keep it dry. Walking back to the mouth of the cave, he was momentarily blinded as a bolt of lightning struck a tree just ten feet from where he was standing. He could feel the bolt’s heat and both hear and smell the wood sizzling as it burned. His skin tingled with electricity. Going back out there was definitely not an option.

Unpacking his sleeping bag, he found a nice little spot to get some sleep for the night. Aside from the recent excitement of the storm and the rush to get to the cave, he was pretty exhausted from the long day. Closing his eyes, he decided he would explore the cave another day.

Drifting off to sleep he dreamed of all of the women he had seduced over the years. Blondes, brunettes, redheads. Whites, blacks, Latinas, Asians. The variations of women seemed almost unending. Never once had he slept with the same woman a second time and some of them he had slept with at the same times. His life had been one long wet dream. But, somehow, this dream still managed to return to his one enduring fantasy woman.

Carrina.

Instead of dreaming about seducing her, he dreamed about falling in love with her. His unconscious mind envisioned living a long life with her by his side: getting married, having kids, grandkids, even great-grandkids. He was in love with her even if his conscious mind was unwilling to admit it.

 

Into the Darkness

Early the next morning J.J. was awakened by a violent crash of thunder and lightning. The heady aroma of charred wood filled his nostrils. Looking outside he saw that the rain was still falling pretty hard. All of this rain and lightning made it impossible to go out and enjoy the forest and the lake. He decided that while he was trapped in the cave he might as well do some spelunking and explore its hidden mysteries.

Having gone caving before, he had come on this trip semi-prepared. With the exception of a helmet, he pulled all of the gear he would need out of his backpack along with several granola bars and his half-full canteen. Looking outside one last time, he turned and pressed forward into the darkness.

Step by careful step he made his way through the cave’s labyrinthine twists and turns. As bright as his lantern was, it still barely illuminated the path before him. The darkness seemed to ooze from the walls. Having traveled more than half a mile into the cave system, he prayed that he would be able to find his way out again when he was done exploring. After pushing through several squeezes, traversing up and down a few steep dips, and leaping across a couple of pits, he looked at his watch and saw that he had been underground for more than five hours already.

Sitting down on a nearby boulder, he set his lantern on the ground beside him. It wasn’t until the lantern began to slide away from him that he realized how close he was to a cliff’s edge. While snacking on a granola bar he shone his flashlight all around him. Not seeing any continuation to the path beyond the canyon, he soon understood that he would either have to turn back in defeat or climb down the cliff face into the light gobbling blackness.

He didn’t know why, but he felt like something was calling to him and he chose to press forward into the unknown.

As he readied his gear for the descent, he began to think about what Carrina would say if she knew what he was doing. He knew that she was very protective of him almost like a step-mom or a big sister – or a wife, but he usually just pushed that thought aside and continued what he was doing. This time, he made sure to check everything two and three times just to appease his thoughts about her protectiveness.

Flashlight in hand, he peered over the edge hoping the drop was not too deep and quickly found that the beam was not going to reach the bottom. He was elated to see that they landed about ten feet away from each other and only about sixty feet down. He had more than enough rope to get down there.

Having finished his granola bar and rehydrated from his canteen, he prepared for his descent. Slipping into his climbing harness and tightening the straps he anchored the rope to the boulder he had just been sitting on. Carefully lowering himself over the edge, he began repelling down the short distance. Even with his lantern strapped to his harness and his head lamp illuminating the way, the darkness continued to feel very oppressive. Down he went being very careful not to miss a foothold. In less than ten minutes he had reached the bottom of the cliff right between the two glowsticks.

Unclipping the lantern from his harness, he looked around and discovered that his travel options were limited. Only one way to go. Less than five hundred yards along the path he came to a dead end. There was nowhere left for him to go. The walls were too smooth to climb without a rope and he had used all of that just getting down here. J.J. sat down and leaned against the dead end wall feeling defeated.

As he sat there in the silent darkness he cursed the probability of his defeated return to the surface. He was unaccustomed to defeat. In the stillness of the cave he began to hear what sounded like water dripping into a pool. It was coming from behind him. But how could that be possible, he thought. This is solid rock. Shining his flashlight all along the wall he had just been leaning against, he found it. Another passage. A way forward. Thirty feet above him was a small opening that might be just big enough for him to squeeze through. Now the question was, how was he going to reach it?

Scanning the wall beneath the passageway one more time, he found what he was hoping for. An inch wide cleft that traveled all the way up past the opening. The challenge; it was six feet to the right of the opening. Also, he did not have any chalk powder for his hands nor was he wearing the right shoes for climbing. But, as it was a short climb, he decided to go for it anyway.

Squeezing four fingers of each hand into the cleft, he began his ascent. His boots had a hard time getting a grip on the smooth rock wall, but, slowly, he made his way up. By the time he reached the thirty foot mark his fingers were screaming with pain.

Looking to his left he saw that the opening was nearly within reach. Carefully removing his left hand from its gripping point in the cleft, he reached out trying to get to the opening. He could just reach the corner of the passage, but the rock was too slick for him to get a good grip. Pointing his head lamp toward the opening, he saw near the center a small lip that might work as a hand hold. If he could reach it.

Gathering his remaining strength, he released his tenuous grip on the cleft and leaped toward the opening. Arms outstretched. The short leap seemed to go in slow motion as he flew through the air. His tired and aching fingers preparing to grasp the small lip. Just as he thought he was going to miss his chance and fall thirty feet back to the path below, his fingers found their mark and gripped the lip. Using the last of his dwindling strength, he pulled himself up into the passage. He had to unclip his lantern and push it in ahead of himself; otherwise he would not have fit. He soon realized that he was going to have to inch himself along to the end as there was no room for any big pushing or pulling movements, much less deep breaths.

Inch by tortuous inch, J.J. made his way through the passage. His energy dwindling fast, he began to think about Carrina again. In his exhaustion he began to picture himself entombed within this passage, never being able to see her again and tell her how much he loved her. I have to make it out of here.

Thirty minutes later, and completely exhausted, he reached the other end of the passage. Pulling himself out of the stone birthing canal he leaned against the wall opposite the opening and tried to regain some of his flagging strength. Maybe, once he regained his strength, he would be able to find the source of the dripping water and refill his nearly empty canteen.

As he sat there snacking on his granola bar and drinking the remainder of his small water supply he began to hear it again. The dripping water. It was much louder now and sounded like it was very close.

Looking in the direction of the dripping sound he noticed something odd on the cave wall. A faint rippling shimmer. But where was the light coming from?

Standing, he walked toward the shimmer. It was a very short walk. Turning a rounded corner, he was awed by what he found. A massive cavern spread out before him. At the center of the cavern was a glowing crystal clear lake. This glow made his use of a lantern look like a flea holding a candle up to the sun.

As he made his way down from his perch above the lake, the path became easier as steps had been carved out of the rock. With each step his awe and wonder grew. The beauty of the crystal clear water. The majesty of the regal rock formations. Then there was the golden statue at the center of the lake.

Standing by the side of the lake, he gazed upon the statue. It was about twenty feet tall and made of solid gold. The subject of the statue was a woman whose physical beauty would have shamed even the most beautiful of supermodels.

The statue somehow reminded him of Carrina. Her beauty had stolen his heart even from their first meeting. Her dark chocolate brown hair which hung in loose curls over her narrow shoulders. Her emerald green eyes which shimmered with the golden flakes that highlighted them. Her sensual lips, often painted a deep crimson, always smiling. Her graceful neck and shoulders, her perfect grapefruit sized breasts, her naturally thin waist and rounded hips and butt, and finally her long slim legs that seemed to go on for miles. But this statue overshadowed even her.

Underneath the golden woman was a brightly jeweled golden pedestal upon which she stood forever frozen. Etched into the pedestal were characters, probably words, but he would have to get a lot closer before he would be able to read them.

Time for a refreshing swim.

Since he was all alone in this subterranean paradise, he had no problem stripping down to nothing for his swim out on the lake. Piling his clothes near the shore, he dove into the water. Surprisingly, the water was actually quite warm and he felt a strange tingling in his skin as he swam.

When he had reached the small island at the center, he climbed out of the water and began examining the golden woman and the pedestal upon which she stood. Seeing the woman’s face this close up, he noticed an uncanny resemblance to Carrina. He had to be hallucinating. There is no way this could be Carrina, he thought. Why would there be a golden statue of Carrina in the middle of this underground lake?

Pushing the thought aside, he stepped back and looked at the statue as a whole. But, it was the writing on the pedestal that intrigued him. It was not Latin, but rather a very old form of ancient Greek. He had not studied the language at all so he gave up on translating it, but he did recognize some of the characters as Greek. My college fraternity had been good for something after all, he thought. He was still very curious about what it said though.

Knowing that he had the next three weeks in which to study the statue and the cave, he decided that it was about time for him to head back to camp. Tilting his head back under the golden pitcher, he let several drops of water plunge into his mouth before he swallowed. From the very first drop he knew there was something special about this water. Not only was it as sweet as honey straight from the honeycomb, but it had a certain essence about it that infused him with energy from head to toe.

Diving back into the water, he swam back to his pile of clothes on the shore. Before leaving the cavern he refilled and emptied his canteen twice over. Finally sated, he dressed and worked his way back out of the cave.

When he reached the mouth of the cave, he saw that it had stopped raining and the clouds had dispersed. Recovering his gear he returned to his campsite. With the exception of the days he had to go fishing to keep himself fed, he spent every day at the underground lake. From that very first taste, he no longer drank anything but the water from the lake. He spent hours gazing at the gorgeous golden woman as he swam naked in the warm tingling waters of the glowing lake. He thought about Carrina every time he looked at the statue’s face. He couldn’t wait to get home to tell her all about it.

By the end of the second week he had begun to notice a general achiness all over his body. When he packed up his gear at the end of the third week, he knew something was wrong. He barely had enough strength to carry his backpack through the forest, let alone the kayak too. So, he left the kayak behind and secured it to a tree for when he returned someday. By the time he had driven home, he felt completely exhausted and his body hurt everywhere.
